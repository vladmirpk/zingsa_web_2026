Space Engineering

Robotics Introductory Programme

Three weeks. Real robots. Practical autonomy.

Three weeks. Real robots. Practical autonomy.

Learn robotics where engineering meets space technology. In this practical introductory programme, you will design, wire and programme a mobile robot, integrate real sensors and motors, and complete an autonomous planetary-rover challenge. Taught by the Space Engineering Department inside Zimbabwe’s national space agency, the course combines essential theory with extensive supervised laboratory work.

Duration3 weeks
FormatPractical labs
LocationHarare
TrackSpace Engineering
ChallengePlanetary rover mission

What you’ll learn

  • Understand the core components and operating principles of robotic systems.
  • Build safe electronic circuits and assemble a mobile robot chassis.
  • Programme a microcontroller to read sensors and control motors.
  • Develop line-following, obstacle-avoidance and basic autonomous behaviours.
  • Test, troubleshoot and present a complete robotics project.

Who it’s for

Senior secondary-school learners, engineering students, apprentices, technicians, teachers and technology enthusiasts — no prior robotics or programming experience required.

You’ll leave with

  • A ZINGSA Certificate of Completion.
  • A completed mobile-robot project and live mission experience.
  • Documented code, circuit diagrams and project evidence for your technical portfolio.

About the programme

Real-world engineering. Hands-on labs. Space technology inspired.

The Robotics Introductory Practical Programme takes learners through design, integration, programming and a final autonomous mission — building the same problem-solving mindset used in satellite systems, UAV operations and national space engineering at ZINGSA.
