Human capital development

Courses that build Zimbabwe’s space workforce

ZINGSA invests in people as powerfully as it invests in satellites and sensors. Structured courses in Space Engineering and GIS — including drone training, CNC machining, 3D printing, and CubeSat development — grow the engineers, analysts, and pilots who carry the national mission forward.

Space Engineering

Hands-on programmes that take learners from satellite fundamentals to flight-ready hardware.

  • CubeSat development and small-satellite assembly
  • 3D printing and rapid prototyping for aerospace parts
  • CNC machining for precision engineering
  • Satellite subsystems, orbital mechanics, and ground segment operations

GIS & Geospatial Sciences

International training

Representing Zimbabwe in Egypt

Ms Patience Muchini of the Space Engineering Department attended the Introduction to Satellite Systems and Applications Course hosted by the Egyptian Space Agency. The programme covered orbital mechanics, satellite subsystems, ground segment operations, and applications in remote sensing and Earth observation.

Her strong grasp of the material and active participation set her apart among the delegates — she was selected to deliver a speech on behalf of all participants. In her address, she reflected on the course experience, spoke to ZINGSA’s growing space programmes and Zimbabwe’s broader space ambitions, and highlighted how satellite systems connect to her own engineering work.
